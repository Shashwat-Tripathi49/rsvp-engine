The Art of Speed Reading

Speed reading is a collection of reading methods which attempt to increase rates of reading without substantially reducing comprehension or retention. Methods include chunking and eliminating subvocalization.

The many available speed reading training programs include books, videos, software, and seminars. There is very little scientific evidence regarding speed reading, and it has generally been found to be ineffective.

What is RSVP?

Rapid Serial Visual Presentation (RSVP) is a scientific method for studying the timing of vision. In RSVP, a sequence of stimuli is shown to an observer at one location in their visual field. This technique has been applied to reading to help people read faster.

Instead of your eyes moving across lines of text, RSVP presents words one at a time in the same location. This eliminates the need for saccades (eye movements) and helps reduce subvocalization, which is the tendency to "say" words in your head as you read.

The Optimal Recognition Point

Research has shown that our eyes focus on a specific point in each word when reading. This is called the Optimal Recognition Point (ORP). By centering each word on its ORP, speed reading tools can help you read more efficiently.

The ORP is typically located near the center of the word, but not exactly at the center. Shorter words have the ORP earlier in the word, while longer words have it positioned further from the beginning.

Practical Tips for Speed Reading

Start with a comfortable speed and gradually increase it as you become more familiar with the RSVP technique. Don't worry about comprehension initially - it will improve with practice.

Use the pause buttons to reread difficult sections. Some content, like technical documentation or dense academic papers, may require a slower pace even with speed reading techniques.

Regular practice is key to improving your reading speed. Try to use speed reading for at least 15-20 minutes each day to build and maintain your skills.

Conclusion

While speed reading is not a magic solution that instantly transforms anyone into a reading machine, it can be a valuable tool for increasing reading speed and comprehension when used correctly.

The key is to practice regularly, start at a comfortable pace, and gradually work your way up to higher speeds. With patience and persistence, you can significantly improve your reading efficiency.
